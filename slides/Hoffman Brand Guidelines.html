20 · proportions
Contrast & accessibility

Contrast is the gate.

Any two brand colors may meet — if the pair clears WCAG. Body text needs 4.5:1. Large (≥ 24px) or bold (≥ 19px) type and UI need 3:1. Below 3:1, colors may only sit block-beside-block, never as type.

The full pair-by-pair matrix lives in the design system · brand-contrast-matrix.html
21 · contrast
Bold pairings · who sits on whom

Every pairing has a size.

Pick the surface first. Then read its column: Body colors (≥ 4.5:1) are safe at any size; Lg+Bold colors (3–4.5:1) carry headlines ≥ 24px, bold ≥ 19px, and UI — never body copy. Everything else stays block-beside-block.

22 · pairings
Color rules

Restraint, always.

Do

Lead with one secondary as the mood, then add a second as accent or type.

Combine any two colors — across the pairs — if they clear WCAG (≥ 4.5 body, ≥ 3 large).

Use any color as a surface, not just navy & paper.

Use lime-600 (#A7BC00) for lime-toned text on white.

Don't

Gradients, three-color washes, glows.

Any color pair below 3:1 used as type.

Lime or cyan as text on white.

All six colors competing at once.

Highlights. The marker pen.

A CSS overlay applied over body text — a brand-color background behind a word, the way a marker pen would. Not an SVG asset; a styling technique. The highlight color comes from the brand palette and must form a WCAG-safe contrast pair with the text color.

37 · highlights
Voice as visual

Emojis as punctuation. Max three per page.

Emojis are part of the brand. Used sparingly, paired with copy, for visual storytelling. Cross-platform variance (Apple, Google, Microsoft, Samsung) is expected and fine — the meaning carries across systems even if the glyph shifts slightly.

Do

Goodbye client rosters. Hello ecosystems 🪐

📂 Available to download here.

We're tripling down on video 📽️

Emoji replaces or extends a word — it does work, not decoration. Max 3 per surface. Counts within the 1–2 annotations per surface budget.

Don't

🚀 💡 🎯 🔥 💪 — emoji stacks as decoration.

🚀 Revolutionary disruption 🚀

Click here 👇 to learn more 👇

No stacks, no bookends, no "click here" affordances. Don't pair with agency clichés. Don't lean on emoji to carry meaning the copy hasn't earned.

VS. PICTOGRAMS Unicode pictograms (→ ✓ ★ · —) are text glyphs, not images — they inherit text color and weight. Use them as typographic affordances inside copy (a link arrow, a checkmark in a sentence). Distinct from emojis: pictograms always look the same; emojis vary by platform.

Voice as a system

How we sound. Phrases we lean on.

The brand voice has signature moves. Use them to keep copy distinctively Hoffman — short, opinionated, a little playful, never corporate.

PATTERN 01 · STRETCHED LETTERS

gooooodbye 2025
HAvvvvve you met

Stretch a letter for hello / goodbye / surprise. Cover and section slides only — never in body copy.

PATTERN 02 · THE LADDER
In 2026 we're
leaning in
more
more
more
into our influence in tech.

Step a word up or down in scale to make commitment visible. The repetition is the point.

PATTERN 03 · STRIKETHROUGH HUMOR
We're doubling tripling down on video.
Goodbye Hello, ecosystems 🭐

Cross out the safer word; replace with the bolder one. Treats the slide like a draft, mid-edit. Honest, energetic.

DO

Short. Opinionated. Lowercase first words for casual signals (hellllllo, hoffman has always been a challenger brand). Drop pronouns to sharpen (Built for tech. Human at heart.). Use a question to open (Why now?). Lead with a verb (Bringing calm and order to Jensanity).

DON'T

Stretched letters in body copy. The ladder pattern more than once per deck. Strikethrough on every page (it loses punch). Title Case Every Word. Agency clichés — leverage, end-to-end, at the intersection of, best-in-class.
