# The Hoffman Agency — Portable Brand System for Social Tiles
*(Paste this whole document into Lovart. It is self-contained — no external files needed. Optimized for social media: LinkedIn carousels, IG squares, IG stories. For these formats, default to bold COLOR surfaces and visual storytelling — the paper/white editorial look is deprioritized.)*

## 1 · Who we are
The Hoffman Agency is an integrated communications agency for tech brands. The brand is smart, human, energetic, distilled, bold, creative, authentic. It looks like a sharp editor's desk: huge confident type, flat color blocks, hand-drawn marker annotations, square corners, lots of air. It never looks like generic tech marketing: no gradients, no glows, no 3D, no stock-photo gloss.

## 2 · The ten non-negotiables
1. One tile = one idea. A single hero statement, set huge. Everything else shrinks hard.
2. Headlines are bold Poppins with exactly ONE word/phrase in Libre Baskerville ITALIC — this contrast is the brand's signature type move.
3. Flat solid color backgrounds only. NO gradients, ever. A "gradient" is replaced by two colors split with a hard edge.
4. Square corners (0–2px radius) on every block, frame, and button. Pill shapes only for tiny single-word tags.
5. Max 1–2 hand-drawn annotation marks per tile (underline, circle, arrow, tick, cross, or marker highlight). They are editorial punctuation, not decoration.
6. Type colors must pass contrast: body text ≥ 4.5:1 against its background; large/bold display type ≥ 3:1. Never lime or cyan text on white.
7. No icons as decoration. No 3D renders, no glassmorphism, no drop shadows, no glow, no lens flares, no motion blur.
8. Sentence case everywhere. UPPERCASE only for small eyebrow labels with wide letter-spacing.
9. Max 3 emojis per tile, and only replacing/punctuating a word in copy ("Hello, ecosystems 🪐") — never as bullets or decoration.
10. Generous margins. Minimum 80px padding on a 2160px tile; the design must breathe.

## 3 · Color — surfaces first
For social tiles, the background IS the design. Pick ONE brand color as the full-bleed surface per tile; rotate surfaces across a carousel so the set feels alive.

Palette (exact hex):
- Deep Navy #182D43 — primary. The workhorse dark surface.
- Lime #D2EB00 — primary accent. Electric, used in short bursts; also a valid light surface.
- Lavender #CB65FF — secondary surface, high-energy.
- Purple #6103B9 — secondary surface, confident editorial pause.
- Cyan #86FFF1 — secondary LIGHT surface, crisp.
- Teal #145F7B — secondary dark surface, credible and calm.
- Paper #FAFAF7 / White #FFFFFF — for social, use sparingly (an occasional "breather" tile in a carousel, max 1 in 5).

What type may sit on each surface (pre-validated, follow exactly):
- On Navy: white (body), Lime #D2EB00 (any size), Cyan #86FFF1 (any size), Lavender #CB65FF (large/bold only).
- On Teal: white (body), Cyan (any size), Lime (any size).
- On Purple: white (body), Cyan (any size), Lime (any size), Lavender (large/bold only).
- On Lavender: Navy (body), Purple #6103B9 (large/bold only), white (large/bold only — prefer navy).
- On Cyan: Navy (body), Teal #145F7B (any size), Purple (any size). NEVER white on cyan.
- On Lime: Navy only (body and display), Purple (large/bold). NEVER white on lime.
- On Paper/White: Navy (body), Purple (any size), Teal (any size), dark lime #A7BC00 for lime-toned accents. NEVER bright lime #D2EB00 as text on white.

Combination rules: one dominant surface color per tile, plus at most one accent color in the type (e.g. navy surface, white headline, the italic emphasis in lime). Cross-pollinating warm and cool (cyan on purple, lime on teal) is encouraged — the pairs above already pass contrast. Never let three or more colors compete on one tile.

## 4 · Typography
- Workhorse: Poppins (geometric sans). Headlines in Bold/ExtraBold (700–800) with tight letter-spacing (about -2%). Body in Regular.
- Emphasis: Libre Baskerville, ITALIC ONLY. Never upright. Exactly one word or short phrase per headline renders in Baskerville italic, usually in the accent color. Choose the word with the most meaning or sound: trust, influence, clarity, judgment, attention, people.
- Small labels ("eyebrows"): Poppins Bold, small, UPPERCASE, letter-spacing about +14%, often with a small lime dot before them.
- Numbers/stats/metadata: JetBrains Mono (or any clean monospace).
- Scale on a 2160×2160 tile: hero headline 140–260px; supporting line 48–64px; eyebrow 36–44px. One hero idea per tile — if everything is big, nothing is.

Signature voice-as-type moves (use one per carousel, not per tile):
- Stretched letters on cover tiles: "hellllllo 2026", "gooooodbye noise". Casual confidence. Never in body copy.
- The ladder: a word stepping up or down in size across stacked lines ("more / more / more"). Repetition is the point.
- Strikethrough humor: cross out the safer word, hand-drawn, replaced by the bolder one ("we're ~~doubling~~ tripling down"). Once per carousel max.
- Lowercase first words are an accepted casual signal ("hoffman has always been a challenger brand").

## 5 · Voice & copy
Short sentences. Then a longer one to breathe. Lead with the punchline. Active voice, "we" for the agency, "you" for the reader. Confident claims over clever puns. CTAs are direct verb + object: "See the work", "Tell us your story" — never "Learn more".
Banned words: synergies, leverage, end-to-end, at the intersection of, best-in-class, revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive, storytelling solutions, unlock.
Brand-true sample lines: "Complexity out. Clarity in." · "Less noise. Sharper signal." · "Built for tech. Human at heart." · "We turn opinion into influence." · "Counsel without conviction is just consulting."

## 6 · Signature graphics
THE STORYLINE SQUIGGLE — the brand's only "wavy" element. A single hand-drawn waveform line that rises left-to-right with small bumps building to one tall peak near the right, then dropping off. It is a story arc, not a chart. Two legal uses:
1. On dark/colored surfaces (navy, purple, teal): draw the line in Lime #D2EB00, thick stroke, as a BACKGROUND layer behind the type, scaled to full tile height and anchored flush to the top, right and bottom edges. Content sits on top of it.
2. On light surfaces: a small square lime badge in a corner containing the squiggle in navy — like a logo mark.
Never: recolor the line away from lime, put it on a lime background, use it as a divider, tile it, rotate it, or float it small in the middle of the canvas.

HAND-DRAWN ANNOTATIONS — marker-pen marks drawn over/under the type, slightly imperfect, 3–6px stroke, round caps: single/double underlines, loose ellipses circling a word, curved arrows, ticks, cross-outs, small scribble accents, and a marker HIGHLIGHT (solid color bar painted behind a word, e.g. lime behind navy text). Pick the mark color for maximum contrast against both the background and the word it touches. Max 1–2 marks per tile.

NO other decorative linework: no extra wavy lines, no squiggly arrows beyond the annotation set, no geometric pattern fills.

## 7 · Composition for social formats
- Square tile 2160×2160 (LinkedIn/IG): full-bleed color surface; asymmetric layout — type biased to the left or top ~60–70%, deliberate negative space in the rest. Carousel series number small in the top-left in mono or Poppins Bold.
- Story 1080×1920: same rules, hero statement in the vertical center third, eyebrow top-left, CTA near bottom.
- Carousel logic: rotate surfaces tile-to-tile (e.g. navy → lavender → teal → lime → purple), keep the type system identical so the set reads as one voice. Cover and closing tiles may carry the squiggle and the logo; middle tiles carry NO logo.
- Logo (when used): the wordmark "Hoffman Agency" — white version on navy/purple/teal/lavender, navy version on lime/cyan/paper. Bottom-left, small. Never stretched, never recolored beyond these.

## 8 · Photography (when a tile uses an image)
Candid, documentary, natural light, business-casual people, warm color grade, eye-level. Never staged stock poses, suits, cool-blue grading, or 3D abstractions. If type sits on a photo, flood it with a 60–75% Deep Navy overlay first — type never sits on a raw image. Black-and-white is allowed for portrait gravitas only.

## 9 · Hard bans (repeat in every generation)
NO gradients. NO drop shadows or glows. NO rounded cards. NO frosted glass. NO 3D chrome or meshes. NO decorative icons. NO wavy lines except the one Storyline squiggle. NO lime or cyan text on white. NO white text on cyan or lime. NO more than 2 annotation marks. NO logo on mid-carousel tiles. NO all-caps paragraphs. NO stock-photo clichés.
